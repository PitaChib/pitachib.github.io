## Genomic Architecture of Local Adaptation Along the Manu Elevational Gradient

The Andes represent one of the most dramatic environmental gradients on Earth. My dissertation characterizes the genomic basis of local adaptation in small mammal communities across the Manu elevational gradient (Peru), integrating both historical museum specimens and contemporary genomic data.

## Urban Adaptation in Rodents Using µCT Scans 
Mentor: [Dr. Sharon Jansa](https://cbs.umn.edu/jansa-lab)
**Abstract**
Urban environments are novel and challenging environments for most wildlife species, yet many successfully adapt and thrive through behavioral and physiological adaptations to urban settings. In Minnesota, white-footed mice (*Peromyscus leucopus*) from urban areas show larger cranial capacities than their rural counterparts, suggesting potential selection for cognitive abilities. However, previous studies estimated brain size using external skull measurements, and their accuracy in predicting actual brain volume remains uncertain. To address this, we used µCT scans to generate digital brain endocasts from museum specimens (*P. labecula* [n = 9], *P. gambelli* [n = 1], *P. maniculatus* [n = 4], *P. melanotis* [n = 1], *P. sonoriensis* [n = 1]). By comparing endocast volumes with traditional skull measurements, we assessed their predictive power and identified the best linear measurements for estimating brain size. Using an AIC model averaging approach, we developed a predictive model to estimate brain volume from external measurements. Our results indicate differences in brain volume between urban and rural populations; however, in the interaction term model, only the rural population exhibits a significant allometric effect. While the difference in slope is not statistically significant, our findings highlight the need for higher-resolution data to clarify the relationship between urbanization and brain size in genus Peromyscus. It remains uncertain whether selection is acting on cognitive abilities or if other factors, such as nutritional availability, contribute to these patterns and require further investigation.
